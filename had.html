Hadrosaurus

Hadrosaurus is a genus of duck-billed dinosaur (hadrosaurid) that lived during the Late Cretaceous period, around 75 to 70 million years ago. It is notable for being one of the earliest hadrosaurids to be discovered and described, and it played an important role in the understanding of this group of dinosaurs.

Facts About Hadrosaurus

  • At First, Scientists Thought that Hadrosaurus Leaned on its Tail, Kangaroo-Style.
  • Paleontologists Briefly Questioned its Status as a Legit Dinosaur.
  • Haddonfield Has a Stream Named After Its Prehistoric Hero
  • Reproduced by laying eggs
  • Existed from 85.8 million years ago to Maastrichtian Age

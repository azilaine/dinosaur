Ankylosaurus

Ankylosaurus is one of the most famous and iconic armored dinosaurs, belonging to the Ankylosauridae family, a group known for their heavily armored bodies and clubbed tails. It lived during the Late Cretaceous period, around 68 to 66 million years ago, and is often recognized for its unique body design, which provided it with excellent defense against predators.

Facts About Ankylosaurus

  • Ankylosaurus had bony plates covering its body, providing protection against predators.
  • Perhaps its most distinctive feature, the tail of Ankylosaurus ended in a large, bony club.
  • It was one of the largest armored dinosaurs, with estimates suggesting it could grow up to 30 feet in length and weigh as much as 6 tons.
